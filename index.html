Early Introduction

My mother taught me to crochet when I was young. We were always learning some kind of craft or skill, but my mother particularly loved to crochet. As a kid, I managed to make several potholders and I started a scarf that only ended up being about twice the length of a potholder before I abandoned it out of frustration. I wouldn't become passionate about this hobby for a couple of decades, but the seed had been planted.

Reintroduction

When I was 27, I relearned to crochet using how-to books. I was scheduled to have surgery and would have several weeks of recovery where I had to keep my feet elevated. I made many, many hats and gave them away as Christmas gifts. My interest in crochet would wax and wane every few years over the twenty years.

Current Projects

During the pandemic, I once again picked up this familiar hobby with gusto. My daughter would send me photos of crocheted tops or accessories and ask me to reproduce them for her.

I also still make hats, but they tend to fit better and be less itchy these days.
